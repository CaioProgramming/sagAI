# Cyberpunk Heavy Augmentation - Visual Reference Guide

## Character Visual Hierarchy

### FLESH vs. MACHINE RATIO

**Minimal Cyberware (Still Too Subtle for New Standards)**

- 70-80% organic flesh remaining
- A few visible augmentations
- ❌ NOT RECOMMENDED under new guidelines

**Standard Cyberpunk (What We're Aiming For)**

- 50-60% organic flesh remaining
- Multiple visible systems: eyes, limbs, facial panels, neural ports
- ✅ GOOD - Characters look clearly augmented

**Heavy Cyberpunk (Strongly Encouraged)**

- 30-50% organic flesh remaining
- Extensive replacement: full limb replacements, facial reconstruction, multiple spinal
  augmentations
- Most remaining flesh shows scarring and integration seams
- ✅✅ EXCELLENT - Clearly more machine than human

**Extreme Cyberpunk (Acceptable for Specialized Characters)**

- 10-30% organic flesh remaining
- Nearly complete mechanical body
- Only head or torso retains any organic structure
- ✅✅✅ POWERFUL - Ultimate dystopian transformation

## Specific Augmentation Details

### EYES (Visual Anchor - Make Them IMPOSSIBLE to Miss)

**Cybernetic Replacement Option 1: Scanner Eyes**

- Large mechanical pupils (3-4x normal size)
- Visible LED arrays in corners
- Glowing reticle HUD grid in iris
- Mechanical eyelids with servo motors
- Circuitry visible around eye socket (not hidden)
- Color: Cold blue, cold amber, deep purple glow
- Effect: Character looks like advanced drone/hunter

**Cybernetic Replacement Option 2: Multi-Lens Eyes**

- Multiple lens arrays visible (like insect compound eyes)
- Nested mechanical rings around iris
- Data streams flowing across pupil
- Rotating mechanical iris diaphragm
- Color: Red scanner sweep, cold white, amber data trails
- Effect: Character looks inhuman and alien

**Cybernetic Replacement Option 3: Blank Tech Eyes**

- Featureless metallic surfaces with slight reflectivity
- Only subtle seams and mechanical detail visible
- Faint glow from within the socket
- Color: Silver with deep purple or blue undertones
- Effect: Truly inhuman—no eyes to connect with

---

### FACE RECONSTRUCTION (Make It OBVIOUS)

**Partial Reconstruction**

- Chrome cheekbone plates replacing organic bone
- Synthetic mandible (jaw) with visible servo motors at the hinge
- Neon circuit patterns glowing across synthetic skin
- Edges where flesh meets chrome: clear integration seams with scarring

**Heavy Reconstruction**

- Half or more of face is synthetic
- Exposed mechanical structure beneath translucent panels (like armor plating)
- Circuit patterns visible as glowing lines under synthetic skin
- Nose may be replaced with tech protrusion/sensory array
- Color: Dark grey, gunmetal with deep purple/blue accent lights

**Example Visual**: Think of a face that's part Terminator, part Ghost in the Shell—mechanical
foundation with enough synthetic skin to suggest they were once human, but now it's impossible to
forget what they've become.

---

### LIMBS (Full Replacement, Not Partial)

**Full Arm Replacement Examples**

- Hydraulic cylinder visible at shoulder and elbow joints
- Carbon-fiber muscle bundles visible between metal framework
- Mechanical fingers (5-6 articulated digits) with exposed tendons/cables
- Glowing power conduits running along forearm
- Multiple colors: Chrome, dark steel, copper tubing, deep purple accent lights
- Asymmetry: Left and right arms may look different (different models, replacement times)

**Full Leg Replacement Examples**

- Piston-driven knee joint (visibly extends/retracts)
- Mechanical thigh with exposed motor housing
- Foot is obviously metal—toes articulate with servo motors
- Cable management running along the leg
- Color: Mix of chrome, matte gunmetal, exposed electronics
- Movement: Stride is rigid, joints have mechanical snap

**Integration Scarring**: Where the limb socket meets remaining flesh—aggressive scarring,
discoloration, visible implant port scars

---

### SPINE & SKELETAL SYSTEM (Intimidating Detail)

**Exposed Mechanical Spine**

- Visible vertebrae segments (chrome/titanium)
- Each segment glows faintly with deep purple/blue
- Connecting cables run along the spine (not hidden)
- Exoskeletal bracing running along both sides of spine/back
- Shoulder-mounted systems attach to this framework

**Rib Cage Augmentation**

- Visible mechanical ribs beneath synthetic skin
- Titanium bone reinforcement showing through gaps in skin
- Chest cavity may have visible internal systems (processors, power supply)
- Breathing may appear unnatural (mechanical/precise rather than organic)

**Effect**: Character's back looks like technological infrastructure, not a human body

---

### NEURAL INTERFACES (Multiple Ports = Higher Status/Specialization)

**Temple Ports**

- Data jack emerging from temple area
- Glowing neural pathways visible beneath thin skin layer
- Multiple connection points (3-6 different jacks)
- Color: Deep purple glow emanating from beneath skin

**Neck Ports**

- Visible thick cables running down neck to body
- Junction boxes at base of skull
- Mechanical collar piece housing the port array
- Scarring radiating outward from connection points

**Spinal Base Ports**

- Large mechanical dock at base of spine
- Cables can physically connect to external systems
- Armor plating protecting the connection
- This is where the character "plugs in" for major data uploads

**Visual Effect**: Character looks wired into the system, not just augmented—they're PART of the
network

---

### DAMAGE & DECAY DETAILS (Critical for Realism)

**Scarring**

- Aggressive raised scars at flesh-chrome interfaces
- Discolored skin around implant sites (purple, grey, necrotic tones)
- Stitched seams where synthetic skin was grafted
- Skin pulling tightly across replacement panels

**Mechanical Wear**

- Rust on budget augmentations
- Scratches and dents on exposed armor
- Mismatched components (different era chrome mixed with newer steel)
- Visible duct tape, jury-rigged repairs holding components together

**Infection/Rejection Signs**

- Necrotic tissue (blackened, dead-looking flesh)
- Visible irritation around implants
- Asymmetry from infections causing partial rejection

**Movement Artifacts**

- Servo joints making audible "tick" or "whir" sounds (suggest in visual)
- Mechanical precision in movement (too smooth, too regular)
- Jerky pauses where organic reaction would be smooth
- Slight delay in response (data processing lag)

## Color Coding for Augmentation Quality

**Military-Grade Augmentation** (Expensive, Functional)

- Polished chrome and gun-metal
- Minimal weathering
- Professional integration
- Usually on specific limbs or systems

**Street-Clinic Augmentation** (Budget, Visible Seams)

- Darker steel, more oxidation
- Visible scarring and jury-rigging
- Asymmetrical, patched-together appearance
- Mix of different era components

**Salvaged/Black Market** (Cheap, Dangerous-Looking)

- Rust and corrosion visible
- Clearly mismatched pieces
- Duct tape and improvised fasteners
- Most aggressive/unstable appearance

## Environmental Integration

**Character in Street**

- Cables trailing from body, connecting to street infrastructure
- Neural interface jacks catching neon glow
- Cyberware reflecting holographic ads
- Body movement synchronizes with environmental tech

**Character in Corporate Interior**

- Sleek military-grade augmentations (less worn, more polished)
- Integration ports designed for corporate systems
- Clean, efficient scarring
- Augmentation aesthetic matches minimalist environment

**Character in Undercity/Slums**

- Maximum visible wear and damage
- Jury-rigged repairs and salvaged components
- Heavy scarring and rejection signs
- Augmentation tells story of survival

## Movement & Body Language

**Heavy Cyberpunk Characters Move Differently:**

- **Rigid Posture:** Back stays straight, shoulders locked (spinal augmentation)
- **Servo Joints:** Audible clicks at elbow/knee (suggest with visual tension)
- **Precise Movement:** Too perfect, too fast, mechanical snap in motion
- **Eyes Track Smoothly:** Mechanical eyes lock and track like surveillance camera
- **Breathing Unnatural:** Chest may not rise/fall organically (if heavily augmented)
- **Hand Movement:** Fingers move with mechanical precision, no hesitation
- **Stillness:** Can hold absolutely still—inhuman composure

## DO's and DON'Ts

### ✅ DO

- Show at least one FULL limb replacement (arm or leg completely chrome)
- Make eyes obviously mechanical with multiple visible components
- Create facial scarring and integration seams that are clearly visible
- Use multiple augmentation types (neural + limb + facial)
- Include gear/clothing that integrates WITH the augmentation (not hiding it)
- Show mechanical wear, rust, and jury-rigged repairs
- Create asymmetry—left and right sides don't match perfectly
- Use deep purple/blue accent lights for implants and neural ports
- Make the character's relationship with technology OBVIOUS in their appearance

### ❌ DON'T

- Hide augmentations under clothing (let them be visible)
- Make replacement limbs look "clean" and "new" (they should look used/worn)
- Create perfectly balanced anatomy (asymmetry = authenticity)
- Use subtle blending where flesh meets chrome (show the SEAMS)
- Give the character too much remaining human skin (60-70% remaining is ideal)
- Make cyberware look like "accessories" rather than fundamental replacements
- Forget about scarring and integration trauma
- Use neon bright colors for augmentation—stick to deep purple, cold blue, amber
- Create symmetrical, perfect-looking synthetic features (imperfection = realism)

## The Core Principle

**Cyberware is not decoration. It's transformation.**

Every augmentation should tell a story:

- **Why did they need this?** (Survival, Combat, Corporate Mandate)
- **How much did it cost?** (Quality/Wear reflects economic status)
- **What did they lose?** (Scarring, Asymmetry, Inhuman appearance)
- **Who installed it?** (Military, Corp, Street-Clinic, Salvaged)

The artwork should make it **impossible to forget** that this character is no longer fully human.
