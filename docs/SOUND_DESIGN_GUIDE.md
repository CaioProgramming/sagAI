# 🎧 SagAI Sound Design Guide

## DJ FX Instruments & Audio Guidelines

> **Philosophy**: Sound should be felt before it's heard. Every audio element must enhance immersion
> without breaking the player's focus. The goal is ambient integration — players should only
> consciously notice the soundscape after extended gameplay, like suddenly realizing a room has been
> quietly perfumed.

---

## 🌊 Core Design Principles

### 1. **Subliminal Resonance**

- Audio should match the *emotional weight* of the narrative, not just the visual theme.
- Avoid sudden, jarring sounds. Use long attack times and gradual fades.
- Layer sounds at low volumes (think -18dB to -24dB below dialogue).

### 2. **Organic Texture**

- Prefer analog imperfections: tape hiss, room reverb, and natural decay.
- Avoid hyper-clean digital sounds that feel sterile.
- Use field recordings and acoustic elements wherever possible.

### 3. **Genre Integration**

- Each genre has a sonic "soul" that must complement its visual art style.
- Sound FX should reinforce the narrative medium (oil painting = warm; pixels = lo-fi).

### ⚔️ **FANTASY** — *Divine Ethereal & Epic Adventure*

> **Art Style**: Classical Oil Painting (High Baroque Period)

#### Sonic Soul

A world of luminous grandeur. The sound of light cascading through clouds and the weight of an epic
journey. It should feel divine, soaring, and spiritually expansive.

#### Key Instruments & FX

| Category             | Instruments / Sounds                         | Notes                             |
|----------------------|----------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------|
| **Melodic Base**     | Full Orchestral Strings, French Horns        | Large, sweeping, heroic melodies  |
| **Harmonic Texture** | Arpeggiated Harp, Celestial Choral Stacks    | Shimmering, light-filled textures |
| **Atmospheric Pads** | Glassy Synth Pads, "Ethereal" Breath Pads    | Floating, weightless feeling      |
| **Divine Accent**    | Crystal Bowls, High- रजिस्टर Bell Chimes     | Sparkling, divine resonance       |
| **Epic Elements**    | Distant Timpani (warm), Cinematic Percussion | Soft but grand sense of scale     |
| **Low-End Tension**  | Warm Cello Ensembles, Low Woodwinds          | Grounded but noble                |

#### Signature FX

- **Ascension Swell**: A rising orchestral crescendo that feels like reaching a mountain peak.
- **Divine Halo**: A shimmering, circular reverb effect around melodic leads.
- **Eternal Breeze**: A soft, harmonic wind effect that carries choral whispers.

#### Forbidden Sounds

- Dark, muddy drones
- Harsh brass stabs
- Anything small-scale or claustrophobic

### 🌆 **CYBERPUNK** — *High Tech, Low Life*

> **Art Style**: Vintage 1980s Cel Animation (Acetate & Ink)

#### Sonic Soul

The hum of a dying city. Neon bleeding through rain-soaked streets. Human decay masked by synthetic
beauty.

#### Key Instruments & FX

| Category             | Instruments / Sounds                              | Notes                                           |
|----------------------|---------------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------|
| **Melodic Base**     | Analog Synths (Juno-106, Prophet-5)               | Warm, slightly detuned. Avoid modern wavetables |
| **Rhythmic Pulse**   | Roland TR-808, LinnDrum                           | Lo-fi drum machines with tape saturation        |
| **Atmospheric Pads** | FM Synth Pads (DX7-style)                         | Glassy, slightly dissonant textures             |
| **Urban Texture**    | Rain on metal, distant sirens, neon buzz          | Field recordings of city decay                  |
| **Accent FX**        | VHS glitches, tape stop effects, bit-crushed hits | Analog degradation                              |
| **Low-End Dread**    | Moog bass drones, sub-harmonic pulses             | Oppressive, claustrophobic                      |

#### Signature FX

- **Neon Halation**: A warm, blooming synth that rises and fades like neon flickering.
- **Chrome Echo**: Metallic delay with decreasing fidelity (tape saturation on repeats).
- **Flatline Drone**: A cold, flat tone that signals danger or loss.

#### Forbidden Sounds

- Clean, modern EDM synths
- Pristine reverbs or delays
- Bright, optimistic tones

### 👻 **HORROR** — *Psychological Dread*

> **Art Style**: 32-Bit Retro 3D Render (PS1 Era)

#### Sonic Soul

The uncanny valley of early digital horror. Sound should feel degraded, unstable, and hauntingly
familiar.

#### Key Instruments & FX

| Category              | Instruments / Sounds                             | Notes                                              |
|-----------------------|--------------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------|
| **Melodic Base**      | Detuned Piano, Music Box                         | Unsettling, off-key, with mechanical imperfections |
| **Rhythmic Pulse**    | Irregular Heartbeats, Ticking Clocks             | Organic but unsettling timing                      |
| **Atmospheric Pads**  | Granular Ambiences, Reversed Reverbs             | Glitchy, unpredictable textures                    |
| **Environmental**     | Dripping water, creaking wood, distant footsteps | Isolation and decay                                |
| **Digital Artifacts** | Sample Rate Reduction, Bit-crushing              | PS1-era audio limitations                          |
| **Tension Builder**   | Shepard Tones, Drone Clusters                    | Never-resolving tension                            |

#### Signature FX

- **Vertex Jitter**: Unstable, warbling tones that feel like corrupted game audio.
- **Flashlight Cone**: Sound narrows and becomes more intimate during tense moments.
- **Polygon Whisper**: Low-fidelity voice samples, almost imperceptible.

#### Forbidden Sounds

- Jump-scare stingers (use tension buildup instead)
- Clear, high-fidelity audio
- Any sound that feels modern or polished

The rhythm of the city. Modern, youthful, and kinetic. NYC/Brooklyn vibes where hip-hop meets

#### Key Instruments & FX

| Category           | Instruments / Sounds                         | Notes                           |

## 🎚️ Implementation Guidelines

### Volume Hierarchy

1. **Dialogue/Narration**: -6dB (always audible)
2. **Music/Ambient**: -18dB to -24dB (subliminal presence)
3. **Sound FX**: -12dB to -18dB (punctuation, not distraction)

### Transition Behaviors

- Use **crossfades** (minimum 2 seconds) between all audio states.
- Match tempo changes to narrative pacing — never jar the player.
- Implement **ducking** for ambient sounds when dialogue plays.

### Dynamic Adaptation

- **Tension Levels**: Build slowly over multiple narrative beats, not suddenly.
- **Release**: After climactic moments, allow 3-5 seconds of near-silence before new audio.
- **Looping**: Use seamless loops with variation (at least 3-4 minute cycles to prevent repetition
  detection).

### Technical Specifications

- **Format**: AAC or OGG (for compression without harsh artifacts)
- **Sample Rate**: 44.1kHz (standard, avoids aliasing)
- **Bit Depth**: 16-bit (sufficient for ambient work)
- **Normalization**: -3dB peak, -18dB LUFS (for consistency and headroom)

## 🎯 Testing Criteria

### The "10-Minute Test"

- Play for 10 minutes without consciously thinking about audio.
- If you notice the sound design, it's too prominent.
- If you feel more immersed than expected, it's working.

### The "Mute Test"

- Play the same scene with audio muted.
- If the experience feels significantly diminished, the audio is integrated correctly.
- If it feels the same, the audio isn't contributing enough.

### The "Genre Swap Test"

- Play audio from one genre against a different genre's visuals.
- It should feel immediately wrong — this confirms genre-specific soul.

---

## 📝 Notes for DJ FX Integration

When implementing these sounds in DJ FX, remember:

1. **Layer Intelligently**: Use 2-3 ambient layers maximum to prevent muddiness.
2. **Randomize Subtly**: Add slight variations to loops (reverb tails, volume micro-changes).
3. **Context Awareness**: Audio should respond to narrative state (tension, calm, action).
4. **Battery Conservation**: Use efficient audio formats and avoid excessive processing.
