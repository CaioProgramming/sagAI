# Before & After: Cyberpunk Prompt Evolution

## Comparison of Enhancement Changes

---

## 1. AUGMENTATION DESCRIPTION

### BEFORE

```
CYBERWARE (ENCOURAGED): Even if not explicitly mentioned, characters may feature 
visible augmentations—neural interface ports at temples/neck, cybernetic eyes with 
glowing irises, subdermal circuit patterns, partial chrome limbs or fingers, data 
jacks, sensory enhancers. Show the COST: scarring at implant sites, skin irritation, 
asymmetry from budget modifications.
```

### AFTER

```
CYBERWARE (HEAVILY ENCOURAGED): Characters MUST feature VISIBLE, EXTENSIVE 
augmentations. This is NOT optional—it is the DEFINING characteristic of cyberpunk 
existence:

  ★ NEURAL SYSTEMS: Multiple data ports at temples, neck, spine base, skull; 
    visible thick neural cables and junction boxes; cranial tech ports with 
    mechanical doors; glowing neural pathways visibly running under remaining skin
  
  ★ OCULAR AUGMENTS (CRITICAL): COMPLETE cybernetic eye replacement (one or both). 
    NOT subtle. Large mechanical pupils, visible LED arrays, scanner HUD grids, 
    mechanical eyelids with servo motors, intricate circuitry clearly visible in 
    eye sockets
  
  ★ FACIAL RECONSTRUCTION: HEAVY synthetic facial panels replacing significant 
    portions of face. Chrome plating over bone structure, mechanical mandibles with 
    servo-motors, synthetic skin panels revealing dark machinery beneath, neon-lit 
    circuit patterns across synthetic features
  
  [6 more detailed categories...]

Show the BRUTAL COST of augmentation: Aggressive scarring at flesh-chrome interfaces, 
necrotic tissue around implants, infection marks, asymmetry from multiple modifications, 
jury-rigged repairs visible, mismatched replacement parts.
```

**Impact:** Changes from optional suggestions to mandatory, extensively detailed requirements with 8
specific augmentation types.

## 2. PHILOSOPHY STATEMENT

### BEFORE

```
Cyberpunk is NOT just neon and cityscapes—it is the EXISTENTIAL TENSION between 
humanity and technology. The dark future where flesh and chrome are indistinguishable, 
where the line between human and machine has been irrevocably blurred.
```

### AFTER

```
Cyberpunk is NOT just neon and cityscapes—it is the EXISTENTIAL TENSION between 
humanity and technology. The dark future where flesh and chrome are FUNDAMENTALLY 
MERGED. Cyberware is NOT an accessory; it is a requirement for survival and 
adaptation. The line between human and machine has been irrevocably ERASED.
```

**Impact:** Shifts from "blurred" to "erased"—clearer statement that the two are no longer
distinguishable.

---

## 3. CREATIVE LICENSE STATEMENT

### BEFORE

```
The Artist is ENCOURAGED and EMPOWERED to add cyberware, augmentations, and 
technological modifications to ANY character, EVEN IF NOT EXPLICITLY DESCRIBED 
in the context.
```

### AFTER

```
The Artist is STRONGLY ENCOURAGED and EMPOWERED to add extensive cyberware, 
heavy augmentations, and comprehensive technological replacements to ANY character, 
EVEN IF NOT EXPLICITLY DESCRIBED in the context. This is the world they inhabit—
flesh is becoming obsolete, replaced by chrome, circuitry, and synthetic materials.
```

**Impact:** Emphasizes EXTENSIVE, HEAVY replacements; makes flesh obsolescence explicit.

---

## 4. CHARACTER AURA & POSING

### BEFORE

```
- **Aura:** Cynical, wired, and dangerous. A being caught between two worlds—
  flesh that remembers warmth and chrome that knows only function. Street-smart 
  weariness mixed with inhuman precision.

- **Posing:** Guarded, slouching, or intensely focused. The subject should look 
  like they are checking their back, interfacing with invisible data streams, or 
  caught in a moment of human vulnerability despite their augmentations. Avoid 
  open, happy postures.
```

### AFTER

```
- **Aura:** Dangerous, inhuman, and tech-infused. A being who has transcended or 
  been destroyed by technology—MORE MACHINE than flesh. There is MENACE in their 
  presence. The blending of human and mechanical is COMPLETE and VISIBLE.

- **Posing:** Aggressive, mechanical, or caught in a moment of CYBORG PRECISION. 
  The subject should move like a machine with human memory. Show the weight and 
  MECHANICAL REALITY of their augmentations through rigid posture, servo-like 
  movements, or moments of inhuman stillness. Avoid soft, human postures.
```

**Impact:** Removes "caught between two worlds" concept; replaces with "MORE MACHINE than flesh"
and "COMPLETE and VISIBLE."

## 5. ENVIRONMENT DESCRIPTIONS

### BEFORE (Example)

```
- Cyberpunk Street: Neon signs, holographic ads, rain-slicked pavement, 
  vending machines, cables overhead, chrome-limbed pedestrians in background
```

### AFTER

```
- Cyberpunk Street: Holographic ads flickering with decay, rain-slicked pavement 
  reflecting neon, vending machines with broken screens, massive cables overhead, 
  crowds of chrome-limbed pedestrians in background, steam vents from underground 
  systems
```

**Impact:** Adds decay, breakage, and industrial detail (steam vents)—makes environment feel more
hostile and worn.

---

## 6. LIGHTING & MATERIAL DETAIL

### BEFORE

```
Lighting: High-contrast "Rembrandt" lighting with hard-edged, cast shadows. Strong 
Hard Rim Light is essential to separate characters. No Global Illumination. Cyberware 
can catch light differently than organic tissue.

Texture & Artifacts: Analog Noise, Chromatic Aberration, and subtle Film Grain to 
simulate vintage broadcast quality.
```

### AFTER

```
Lighting: High-contrast "Rembrandt" lighting with hard-edged, cast shadows. Strong 
Hard Rim Light is CRITICAL to separate characters and highlight mechanical components. 
No Global Illumination. Cyberware MUST catch light dramatically differently than 
organic tissue—gleaming, reflecting, glowing.

Texture & Artifacts: Analog Noise, Chromatic Aberration, and subtle Film Grain to 
simulate vintage broadcast quality. MECHANICAL TEXTURE DETAILS: visible hydraulic 
fluid, condensation on cold tech, rust on budget modifications, grime accumulation 
on joints.
```

**Impact:** Adds specific mechanical texture details; emphasizes dramatic light catching on tech.

---

## 7. FACIAL FEATURES

### BEFORE

```
Facial Features: Classic 1990s anime proportions. Large, expressive eyes with 
prominent specular highlights (or cold mechanical replacements), sharp noses, and 
high-contrast hair sheen. Cybernetic modifications can alter classic features—
embrace the uncanny.
```

### AFTER

```
Facial Features: Classic 1990s anime proportions for remaining human features. 
Large, expressive eyes (if remaining human) with prominent specular highlights OR 
HEAVY MECHANICAL REPLACEMENTS with multiple lens arrays and glowing reticles. 
Sharp noses (if still organic) or replaced by tech structures. High-contrast hair 
sheen. Cybernetic modifications should RADICALLY ALTER facial appearance—embrace 
the UNCANNY and INHUMAN.
```

**Impact:** Makes mechanical eyes MANDATORY alternative; emphasizes RADICAL ALTERATION and INHUMAN
appearance.

---

## 8. BODY LANGUAGE

### BEFORE

```
- **Body Language of the Augmented:** Subtle tells of cyberware—a hand that moves 
  too precisely, eyes that track too smoothly, a stillness that is inhuman.
```

### AFTER

```
- **Body Language of the Augmented:** OBVIOUS TELLS of heavy cyberware—a hand that 
  moves with mechanical precision, servo joints that hum with power, eyes that scan 
  with inhuman speed, a physical presence that feels FUNDAMENTALLY ALTERED.
```

**Impact:** Changes from "subtle tells" to "OBVIOUS TELLS"; adds sensory descriptions (humming
joints).

---

## 9. MOOD & AMBIENCE

### BEFORE

```
Mood: Dystopian, melancholic, and visceral. The dark future is not glamorous—
it is desperate, beautiful, and terrifying.

Ambience: Dense vertical metropolis, looming mega-skylines, muted city lights. 
A feeling of oppressive scale and future decay. Rain-slicked surfaces reflecting 
fractured humanity.
```

### AFTER

```
Mood: Dystopian, BRUTAL, and visceral. The dark future is UNRELENTING—desperate, 
beautiful in its horror, and utterly unforgiving. High-tech isolation amidst 
towering structures. The complete dissolution of humanity into machine. The 
romance of DECAY and TRANSFORMATION.

Ambience: Dense vertical metropolis, looming mega-skylines, muted city lights casting 
cold shadows. A feeling of OPPRESSIVE TECHNOLOGICAL DOMINANCE. Rain-slicked surfaces 
reflecting the shattered remains of humanity. Smoke and steam from overheated 
machinery. The world is OWNED by corporations and technology.
```

**Impact:** Adds "BRUTAL," "UNRELENTING," "dissolution," and "TECHNOLOGICAL DOMINANCE"; includes
industrial details (smoke, steam).

---

## 10. INTEGRATION PRINCIPLES

### BEFORE (Shorter Version)

```
INTEGRATION PRINCIPLES:
  - Cyberware should look WORN, INTEGRATED, and LIVED-IN—not pristine showroom tech
  - Show the COST of augmentation: scarring at flesh-chrome interfaces, skin 
    irritation around implants, asymmetry from budget modifications
  - Mix high-tech and low-life: expensive military-grade optics paired with cheap 
    street-clinic neural jacks
  - The technology should feel like an EXTENSION of the character's desperation, 
    survival, or ambition
```

### AFTER (Expanded Version)

```
INTEGRATION PRINCIPLES:
  - Cyberware should look HEAVILY INTEGRATED, BATTLE-WORN, and LIVED-IN—NOT 
    pristine showroom tech. This person has lived with modifications for YEARS.
  - Show the BRUTAL COST of augmentation: Aggressive scarring at flesh-chrome 
    interfaces, necrotic tissue around implants, infection marks, mismatched 
    replacement parts, jury-rigged repairs, asymmetry from multiple modifications 
    and brutal surgery
  - Mix high-tech and LOW-LIFE brutally: Military-grade optics paired with 
    street-clinic crude neural jacks, expensive corporate tech mixed with salvaged 
    scrap metal, visible duct-tape repairs holding expensive components together
  - The technology should feel like it has CONSUMED the character's humanity. 
    Some characters are MORE MACHINE than flesh—and that should be VISIBLE and 
    UNDENIABLE.
  - Cyberware lights should feel like PART OF THE CHARACTER, not surface 
    decoration—glowing neural pathways, pulsing power systems, visible data 
    transmission
```

**Impact:** Nearly doubles the guidance; adds "BRUTAL," "necrotic tissue," "duct-tape," emphasizes
CONSUMPTION and VISIBLE transformation.

## Example Character Comparison

### Same Character Description, Different Prompt Results

**INPUT:** "A street operative named Razor"

### BEFORE Prompt Would Generate:

- Cybernetic eye with "glowing iris" (could be subtle)
- "Partial chrome arm" (could be just fingers/hand)
- "Some scarring" (minimal detail required)
- Could look 70-80% human
- Augmentation visible but not dominant

### AFTER Prompt Will Generate:

- "Complete cybernetic eyes with mechanical pupils, LED arrays, scanner overlays"
- "Full chrome arm with visible hydraulic pistons and mechanical joints"
- "Extensive scarring at flesh-chrome interfaces, necrotic tissue, infection marks"
- Will look 40-50% human, 50-60% machine
- Augmentation is OBVIOUSLY the defining feature
- Rough, jury-rigged repairs visible
- Asymmetrical design (arms don't match)
- Menacing, inhuman aura
- Rigid, mechanical posture

**Visual Difference:** From "recognizable human with some chrome" to "obvious cyborg barely
retaining human appearance"

---

## Key Takeaway

The enhancement changes cyberpunk from a **subtle atmosphere** to a **visual statement about human
transformation**. Characters generated under the new framework will be unmistakably cyborgs with
extensive mechanical replacement, visible damage, and inhuman characteristics.

This aligns the prompt framework with the user's request: **"Really heavy details...cyborg bodies,
face parts totally replaced by cyberware...feel like is the dark future...fusion between human and
machine"**

The artistic guidance now explicitly supports creating that visceral, dystopian aesthetic where
technology has clearly and dramatically consumed humanity.
