# Cyberpunk Heavy Augmentation - Quick Reference

## TL;DR

### The Core Change

Cyberware is NO LONGER optional or subtle. Characters MUST have HEAVY, VISIBLE augmentations that
make them look MORE MACHINE than FLESH.

---

## Essential Augmentation Checklist

For EVERY cyberpunk character, include AT LEAST 3 of these (use the ★ markers):

✅ **★ NEURAL INTERFACES**

- Multiple data ports (temples, neck, skull)
- Visible glowing neural pathways under skin
- Thick cables and junction boxes

✅ **★ OCULAR AUGMENTS (CRITICAL)**

- Complete eye replacement (one or both eyes)
- Mechanical pupils, LED arrays, scanner overlays
- Mechanical eyelids with servo motors
- CANNOT be subtle

✅ **★ FACIAL RECONSTRUCTION**

- Heavy synthetic panels replacing face sections
- Chrome plating over bone structure
- Mechanical mandible with servo-motors
- Exposed machinery beneath translucent panels

✅ **★ LIMB AUGMENTATION**

- COMPLETE full arm/leg replacement (not just fingers)
- Visible hydraulic systems and mechanical joints
- Carbon-fiber muscle weave
- Glowing power conduits

✅ **★ SPINE & SKELETAL MODS**

- Exposed mechanical spine sections
- Exoskeletal reinforcement down the back
- Visible rib-cage augmentations
- Chest cavity with visible internal mechanics

✅ **★ DERMAL TECH**

- Extensive subdermal implant networks as glowing lines
- Heavy scarification covering large body sections
- Metallic skin patches replacing flesh
- Visible titanium reinforcement through gaps

✅ **★ COMBAT AUGMENTATIONS**

- Retractable mechanical claws/blades
- Shoulder-mounted weapon systems
- Integrated tools emerging from limbs
- Visible cooling vents and power systems

✅ **★ SENSORY ENHANCEMENT**

- Heavy cyber-ears with antenna arrays
- Olfactory tech replacing/altering nose
- Sensor modifications protruding from skin

## Visual Intensity Scale

### Level 1: Minimal (❌ NOT RECOMMENDED)

70-80% flesh, 20-30% chrome

### Level 2: Standard (✅ GOOD)

50-60% flesh, 40-50% chrome

- Full limb replacement (at least one)
- Obvious eye augmentation
- Visible facial synthetic panels
- Multiple neural ports

### Level 3: Heavy (✅✅ EXCELLENT - TARGET THIS)

30-50% flesh, 50-70% chrome

- Multiple full limb replacements
- Extensive facial reconstruction
- Spine visible/augmented
- Heavy scarring throughout
- Asymmetrical design (arms don't match)

### Level 4: Extreme (✅✅✅ POWERFUL)

10-30% flesh, 70-90% chrome

- Mostly mechanical body
- Barely recognizable as originally human
- For specialized combat/specialist characters

## Mandatory Visual Details

**SCARS & DAMAGE** (Don't skip these!)

- Aggressive scarring at flesh-chrome interfaces
- Necrotic tissue (blackened, dead-looking) around implants
- Infection marks showing skin irritation
- Asymmetry from multiple modifications
- Visible duct-tape repairs and jury-rigged patches
- Rust on budget augmentations

**MOVEMENT CLUES**

- Servo joints with mechanical snap/clicking
- Rigid posture (spinal augmentation)
- Precise, inhuman movement
- Eyes track like surveillance camera
- Breathing may look unnatural

**COLOR CODING**

- Military-Grade: Polished chrome, minimal wear
- Street-Clinic: Darker steel, obvious scarring, patched
- Salvaged/Black Market: Rust, corrosion, mismatched pieces, duct tape

## Artist Freedom

**YOU ARE ENCOURAGED TO:**

- Add cyberware even if not explicitly mentioned in character description
- Go HEAVIER on augmentation than character background suggests
- Create asymmetrical designs (mismatched parts = authenticity)
- Show visible damage, wear, and repair
- Mix expensive corporate tech with salvaged street-clinic work
- Make characters look INHUMAN and WRONG in ways that reveal their transformation

This is NOT a constraint—it's CREATIVE LICENSE to reinforce the genre's core identity.

## Environment Integration

Make sure the character's augmentation matches their context:

| Setting              | Augmentation Style        | Visual Cues                                              |
|----------------------|---------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------|
| **Corporate**        | Military-grade, polished  | Clean lines, integrated ports, efficient scarring        |
| **Street/Undercity** | Salvaged, jury-rigged     | Rust, duct tape, visible repairs, heavy scarring         |
| **Combat Zone**      | Battle-worn, asymmetrical | Damage, missing pieces, weapon systems active            |
| **Lab/Ripperdoc**    | In-progress, exposed      | Surgical fresh, intense scarring, incomplete integration |

| Subtle chrome accents                  | OBVIOUS mechanical replacement                    |

| Eyes could be human                    | Eyes MUST be mechanical                           |

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Hiding augmentation under clothing** → Show the chrome!
❌ **Making replacement limbs look "new"** → They should look used/worn
❌ **Perfect symmetry** → Real augmented bodies are asymmetrical
❌ **Blending flesh-chrome smoothly** → SHOW the SEAMS and SCARS
❌ **Using subtle colors for augmentation** → Deep purple and cold blue ACCENTS
❌ **Creating too much remaining flesh** → 50-70% should be chrome/synthetic
❌ **Making cyberware look like accessories** → It's FUNDAMENTAL REPLACEMENT
❌ **Forget scarring** → Every augmentation leaves trauma marks
❌ **Making everyone look identical** → Different models, different eras, different conditions

## Quick Prompt Additions

When describing a cyberpunk character, ALWAYS include:

- At least one full limb replacement
- Visible neural interface ports
- Facial augmentation (eyes minimum, ideally more)
- Scarring and integration seams
- At least one visible sign of wear/repair
- Deep purple or cold blue accent lighting

**Example Language:**

- "Complete cybernetic eyes with glowing reticle HUD"
- "Full chrome arm with exposed hydraulic pistons"
- "Heavy scarring at flesh-chrome interfaces"
- "Synthetic mandible with servo motor visible at jaw hinge"
- "Jury-rigged repairs held together with duct tape and hope"
- "Neural pathways glowing faintly beneath translucent skin panels"

## The Philosophy

**Cyberpunk isn't about technology as enhancement.**
**It's about technology as CONSUMPTION.**

The dark future is where flesh becomes OBSOLETE. Where survival means accepting BRUTAL
TRANSFORMATION. Where remaining human is a VULNERABILITY.

Your artwork should make this UNDENIABLE.

Characters should look like they've been through HELL and come out MORE MACHINE than they went in.

This is the visual language of cyberpunk.
